01 · Capabilities

What you can engage us to do

Everything below exists because a paper needed it first. The estimators are published, the proofs are machine-checked, and the index architecture runs in production — we are offering the workshop, not a slide deck about one.

F
Adversarial-systems & friction analysis
Mapping where competing interests generate structural conflict in a market, protocol, or organisation — and decomposing the resulting friction into alignment, stake, and entropy so it can be measured, compared, and reduced.
Σ
Bespoke systemic-risk indices
ASRI-style composite indices built for your domain: sub-index design, aggregation and weighting, validation against historical stress events, and a live dashboard if you want one. The reference implementation runs at asri.dissensus.ai.
⊢
Formal verification in Lean 4
Machine-checked proofs of the properties your mechanism, protocol, or policy is supposed to have, with assumptions tracked in an explicit ledger. The proof assistant does not accept hand-waving, which is precisely its value.
≠
Red-teaming & robustness evaluation
Adversarial evaluation of multi-agent and agentic AI systems: where incentives break the behaviour you intended, which failure modes are structural rather than incidental, and what they cost.
σ²
Econometric & volatility modelling
GARCH-family event studies (we maintain the open-source GJR-GARCH-X estimator), multi-moment analysis, and dependence-robust inference for markets where textbook assumptions fail.
02 · Engagement models

Three ways to work with the lab

01
Advisory
Standing or ad-hoc access to the lab’s expertise — review of models, methods, or system designs. A sceptical second opinion, with the mathematics attached.
02
Bespoke research
A scoped question gets a scoped study: defined deliverable, agreed timeline, and a written artefact you keep — technical report, working paper, or both.
03
Custom tooling
Indices, dashboards, evaluation harnesses, or estimation packages built for your use case — delivered with source, tests, and documentation, hosted or handed over.
03 · Audience

Who this is for

Typical Funds·Financial institutions·AI labs·Policymakers

The common denominator is a system in which multiple parties optimise against each other, and the cost of misreading that dynamic is material. If that describes your problem, the sector label matters less than you would think. Dissensus is a small lab and takes a small number of engagements at a time, so the research does not stall — that constraint is a feature of the work, not an apology.

04 · The distinction

Paying for work vs funding the programme

Services ≠ Partnership

Two doors, deliberately separate

Services is commercial: you bring a problem, we scope it, you own the deliverable, invoices exist. Partnership funds the open research programme: outputs are public, citable, and belong to everyone. Both keep the lab independent — but only one of them gets you a bespoke deliverable, and only the other produces public goods.
