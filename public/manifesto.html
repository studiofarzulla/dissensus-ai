00 · Abstract

The argument in three moves

This manifesto establishes Adversarial Systems as a formal field of study, pursued through the Adversarial Systems & Complexity Research Initiative (ASCRI), grounded in the Replicator-Optimization Mechanism (ROM) and instantiated through the Axiom of Consent.

The argument proceeds in three stages. First, ROM: a substrate-neutral formalism capturing how complex systems persist through friction rather than equilibrium. Second, the Axiom of Consent as ROM’s metaethical instantiation: delegation necessarily produces friction; friction accumulation is measurable; legitimacy is stakes-weighted voice alignment. Third, the case that these results necessitate a unified field — because the mechanism is isomorphic across political economy, financial markets, cognitive architecture, and cosmological dynamics.

The wager is empirical. If ROM friction decomposition predicts observable outcomes across domains, Adversarial Systems is vindicated as unified science. If not, it is an interesting failure. Either way, the current disciplinary fragmentation is not merely inefficient. It is epistemologically incoherent.

01 · The mechanism

Persistence without equilibrium

Complex systems persist. This is the explanandum. Institutions survive regime changes. Markets absorb crashes. Minds endure trauma. The question is: what mechanism governs persistence?

Traditional answers invoke equilibrium. Economic theory posits price convergence. Political theory assumes consensus formation. Thermodynamics predicts entropy maximisation. These frameworks share an assumption — systems tend towards stable states, and departures are pathological. That assumption is false.

Equilibria are measure-zero in generic dynamical systems. Complex systems do not persist because they reach stability. They persist despite never reaching it. Friction — the continuous pressure against stasis — is not noise to be eliminated. It is the mechanism by which identity is maintained.

A system instantiates the Replicator-Optimization Mechanism if it satisfies three conditions — two selection pressures, and the friction between them:

ρ
Replication pressure
Components face selection for persistence. Continued existence requires active maintenance against dissolution.
σ
Optimisation pressure
Components face selection for fitness. Competitive advantage requires adaptation to environmental demands.
F
Friction dynamics
Replication and optimisation are generically misaligned. The friction function F = f(α, σ, ε) decomposes into alignment, stakes, and epistemic uncertainty.
The dynamics in one line

dpt(τ)/dt = Στ' pt(τ') · φ(τ') · M(τ' → τ) − pt(τ) · φ̄t

where pt(τ) is probability mass on configuration τ, φ(τ) measures fitness, and M(τ' → τ) captures transitions.

The equation does not specify what τ represents. This is not ambiguity — it is generality.

Political systems
τ = governance configurations; ρ = institutional persistence; σ = legitimacy optimisation; F = consent-holding friction.
Financial markets
τ = trading strategies; ρ = capital preservation; σ = return optimisation; F = information asymmetry + infrastructure fragility.
Cognitive systems
τ = belief configurations; ρ = self-model coherence; σ = environmental prediction; F = competing subsystem objectives.
Cosmological dynamics
τ = spacetime density distributions; ρ = localisation maintenance; σ = entropic dissipation; F = expansion–contraction tension.
Theorem — equilibria are measure-zero

Fixed points exist. Systems never reach them.

For generic ROM systems with continuous state space and a non-degenerate fitness landscape, the set of fixed points has Lebesgue measure zero. Equilibrium configurations exist mathematically but are dynamically unattainable.

Corollary: dissensus is structurally inevitable. Systems that appear stable are cycling through quasi-stable configurations faster than observation resolution. What we call “equilibrium” is coarse-grained persistence, not fine-grained stasis.

02 · The foundation

From mechanism to metaethics: the Axiom of Consent

ROM is descriptive. It characterises how systems persist. But complex systems involving agents raise normative questions: who bears the costs of friction? Whose optimisation targets count? How should persistence be distributed? These questions require metaethical grounding. The Axiom of Consent provides it.

The axiom

In any system where agents delegate decision-making authority:

  1. Delegation produces friction. The delegator’s optimisation target (σi) diverges from the delegatee’s (σj) with probability 1.
  2. Friction accumulates measurably. The friction function F = f(α, σ, ε) is computable from observable parameters.
  3. Legitimacy is stakes-weighted voice. The legitimacy of an outcome is voice weighted by what each stakeholder has at risk.
F = σ (1 + ε) 1 + α
Clause two, made operational. Friction rises with stake (σ) and entropy (ε), and falls with alignment (α) — one functional, three measurable primitives, tested in every domain the lab touches.
L(d) = Σi σi vi(d) Σi σi
Clause three. The legitimacy of decision d is stakeholder voice v weighted by stakes σ — those with the most at risk count the most. Quantified in Consensual Sovereignty.
Theorem — belief-transfer under delegation

Delegated consent comes to feel owned.

Let Pj(t) denote a delegatee’s subjective probability that consent “belongs” to them rather than the delegator. Under Bayesian updating with asymmetric evidence availability, dPj/dt > 0 for all t > 0. As delegation duration increases, delegatees come to perceive delegated consent as intrinsic property.

This is not pathology. It is optimal inference given the evidence structure: the delegatee experiences daily reinforcement — they exercise the authority — while the counterfactual evidence, the delegator’s continued consent, is invisible. Belief-transfer is Bayes-optimal on the available data.

Consent reclamation is structurally traumatic. When delegators attempt to reclaim authority — decolonisation, employee exit, divorce — delegatees experience genuine loss: not theatre, not bad faith, but the dissolution of what felt like owned property. This does not make reclamation wrong. It makes friction inevitable.

The legitimacy formula has normative consequences:

Uniform voice fails
Democratic theory’s “one person, one vote” ignores stakes heterogeneity. Stakes-weighting captures what matters.
Technocracy fails
Pure expertise delegation ignores voice entirely. Colonial administration exhibits this: peak sophistication immediately precedes collapse.
Productive dissensus wins
Neither consensus nor gridlock but managed contestation: enough friction to force accountability, not so much that coordination fails.
The Axiom of Consent transforms ROM from descriptive mechanism into normative framework. Friction is not merely observable — it is evaluable. Systems can be judged by whether their friction distribution respects stakes-weighted voice.
03 · The field

Why one field, not seven

The components are now on the table: ROM as mechanism, the Axiom of Consent as metaethics. The synthesis is Adversarial Systems — the formal study of how complex systems persist through friction dynamics.

Definition — the field

Adversarial Systems

The formal study of persistence through friction, with particular attention to:

  1. The ROM formalism and its instantiations across substrates
  2. Consent structures and their friction implications
  3. Stakes-weighted legitimacy and its measurement
  4. Coarse-graining and scale transitions
  5. Empirical validation across domains

The case for disciplinary unification is not aesthetic preference. It is logical necessity — three times over.

Argument I — derivational closure
  1. Political friction requires formalising stakes, alignment, uncertainty.
  2. These parameters are not primitive; they emerge from optimisation dynamics.
  3. Optimisation dynamics require ROM: persistence under selection pressure.
  4. ROM requires entropy as axiom: patterns dissolve unless maintained.
  5. Entropy is dedensification: S ∝ ∫ ρ−1 dV.
  6. Dedensification dynamics imply cosmological structure.

Rigorous formalisation of political friction eventually derives cosmology. This is not scope creep. It is what happens when you refuse to hand-wave.

Argument II — isomorphism

The same mathematics governs how institutions lose legitimacy (consent-friction accumulation), how markets crash (infrastructure-friction propagation), how minds develop trauma (adversarial-training optimisation), and how entropy increases (dedensification under expansion).

Isomorphic problems demand unified treatment. Seven disciplines studying the same phenomenon with incompatible notation is not intellectual diversity. It is coordination failure.

Argument III — predictive power

Cross-domain validation is the strongest test: ROM friction decomposition should predict financial volatility asymmetry, institutional failure timing, therapeutic intervention efficacy, and cosmological observables — from one formalism.

Domain-specific theories cannot make cross-domain predictions. Adversarial Systems can.

The research programme, in four movements:

I · Empirical replication
TARCH-X cryptocurrency study across asset classes; belief-transfer in organisational capture; trauma-as-optimisation in longitudinal developmental studies.
II · Formalisation
Complete lumpability proofs for arbitrary coarse-graining; extend ROM to quantum field theory; derive the Born rule from traversal statistics.
III · Policy application
Climate governance as consent-holding failure; AI regulation as friction-aware design; decolonisation reparations via belief-transfer quantification.
IV · Long-term vision
Adversarial Systems as a recognised field with dedicated journals; friction reframed as constitutive; equilibrium models recognised as measure-zero special cases.
04 · The wager

Falsifiable, on purpose

The manifesto, as published in January 2026, stakes its programme on four claims that can lose.

Four falsifiable claims
  • ROM friction decomposition predicts volatility asymmetry in financial markets. In the multi-moment event study, infrastructure shocks show a directionally larger conditional-variance response than regulatory shocks (point estimate ≈4.9×) — a differential that does not survive dependence-robust inference. Whether the asymmetry is real or an artefact of cross-asset dependence is precisely the open wager.
  • Stakes-weighted legitimacy forecasts institutional crises. Testable: historical regime transitions should exhibit legitimacy erosion preceding collapse.
  • Belief-transfer duration predicts reclamation trauma intensity. Testable: longer delegation correlates with more conflictual separation.
  • Entropy-as-dedensification implies cosmological observables. Testable: the Hubble tension should show directional variance if expansion rate varies with local density.

If these predictions hold across domains, Adversarial Systems is vindicated as unified science. If they fail, the framework is falsified. Either outcome advances knowledge: validation yields a paradigm shift, falsification yields precise boundaries. The current alternative — disciplinary fragmentation with no cross-domain predictions — yields neither.

05 · Conclusion

What follows if friction is constitutive

The argument is complete. ROM provides the substrate-neutral formalism for persistence under friction. The Axiom of Consent instantiates it in metaethics: delegation produces friction, friction is measurable, legitimacy is stakes-weighted voice. Adversarial Systems unifies the results into one field, with cross-domain prediction as the validation criterion.

If systems persist through dissensus rather than despite it, the evaluation criteria change:

Governance
Good governance is not consensus but managed contestation.
Markets
Market health is not volatility minimisation but infrastructure robustness.
Minds
Mental health is not affect stability but adaptive friction between competing goals.
Cosmos
The universe does not decay towards equilibrium. It breathes.
A permission, and a warning

Disciplinary silos can persist. Political theorists can ignore econometrics. Economists can dismiss postcolonial critique. Physicists can treat entropy as settled. This is permitted.

But do not then express surprise when institutions collapse, markets crash, minds break, and cosmological tensions accumulate — all exhibiting identical friction signatures that no single discipline can explain.

The universe is adversarial against itself. So are we. Understanding this is not optional. It is the condition of persistence.
