Adversarial Systems:
A Research Manifesto

Why traditional disciplinary boundaries fail for friction dynamics, and why adversarial systems demand a unified field

Author Murad Farzulla
ORCID 0009-0002-7164-8704
Published January 2026

Abstract

Traditional academic disciplines impose artificial boundaries on phenomena that don't respect them. Adversarial systems—institutions, markets, minds, ecosystems, universes—persist through friction, not despite it. Yet political science studies legitimacy erosion without understanding market microstructure. Finance models volatility without grasping institutional consent dynamics. Cognitive science analyzes trauma without recognizing it as maladaptive optimization. Cosmology treats entropy as thermodynamic bookkeeping, missing its role as dedensification pressure.

This fragmentation isn't just inefficient—it's epistemologically bankrupt. Adversarial systems demand a unified field because the mechanism is substrate-neutral: replication under optimization pressure, with friction constitutive of identity.

If we want to understand how complex systems persist, disciplinary silos must dissolve.

The Problem: When Disciplines Partition Reality

Core Observation

Friction Dynamics Don't Respect Field Boundaries

Consider three observations, each studied by separate academic communities with zero cross-pollination.

I

Political Science

Colonial institutions achieved peak bureaucratic sophistication immediately before decolonization. Legitimacy eroded as competence increased. Why does institutional failure accelerate when expertise deepens?

II

Financial Economics

Cryptocurrency markets exhibit 5.7× larger volatility responses to infrastructure shocks than regulatory shocks. Standard models predict the opposite—information asymmetry should dominate operational capacity.

III

Developmental Psychology

Childhood trauma produces hypervigilance, affect dysregulation, relational instability. The standard framing: "pathology." The structural reality: optimal training on adversarial distributions.

IV

The Pattern

These aren't three problems. They're one problem wearing different costumes. Systems persist through friction accumulation, not equilibrium achievement. Same dynamics. Different vocabulary. Zero synthesis.

The Cost of Fragmentation

  • → Explanatory Failure: Political scientists invoke "legitimacy crisis" without formalizing what legitimacy is. It's stakes-weighted voice alignment.
  • → Methodological Poverty: Finance deploys sophisticated time-series models but treats institutions as exogenous "shocks."
  • → Ontological Confusion: Consciousness researchers debate whether qualia "exist"—a pseudo-problem from reifying gerunds.
  • → Missed Unification: Coarse-graining formalisms apply to both cells→organisms and preferences→institutions. But biologists and political scientists never talk.

Why Adversarial Systems Transcend Disciplines

Substrate Neutrality as Ontological Claim

Definition: A system is adversarial if (1) it comprises agents with competing optimization targets, (2) no agent fully controls outcomes, and (3) persistence requires coordination despite misalignment.

This applies to:

  • → Political systems: Citizens vs. representatives, alignment imperfect, institutions persist through consent management
  • → Markets: Buyers vs. sellers, information asymmetric, prices emerge from friction
  • → Minds: Cognitive subsystems compete for behavioral control
  • → Ecosystems: Species compete, persistence through niche differentiation
  • → AI alignment: Human preferences vs. learned objectives, safety through friction management
The same formalism—Replication-Optimization Mechanism (ROM)—governs all. At scale S, dynamics follow:

dpt(τ)/dt = Στ' pt(τ') · φ(τ') · M(τ' → τ) − pt(τ) · φ̄t

The mathematics is isomorphic. Disciplinary divisions are coordinate choices, not ontological necessities.

Friction as Constitutive, Not Pathological

Traditional frameworks across disciplines treat friction as deviation from equilibrium. This is backwards.

THEOREM

For generic ROM systems, the set of fixed points has Lebesgue measure zero in the state space. Equilibrium configurations exist mathematically but are never reached dynamically.

Corollary (Dissensus is Generative): Institutions don't fail because they lose consensus. They fail when friction accumulation exceeds legitimacy regeneration capacity. Productive friction—stakeholders contesting decisions, forcing justification, demanding transparency—maintains institutional health.

Good governance isn't frictionless technocracy. It's managed dissensus with stakes-weighted voice.

Adversarial Systems as Research Program

Core Principles

1

Methodological Pluralism

Formal modeling, empirical validation, normative analysis, phenomenological grounding. No single method suffices. ROM formalism without empirical grounding is unfalsifiable.

2

Domain Agnosticism

Don't start with "I study politics." Start with: "I study persistence under friction." Seek cross-domain validation: Does legitimacy erosion predict market crashes?

3

Epistemic Humility with Formal Rigor

Cosmological extension is speculative. Belief-transfer needs clinical validation. Uncertainty doesn't justify vagueness. Say "We conjecture..." But still write the equations.

4

Friction as Normative Lens

Traditional: minimize friction. Adversarial systems: What friction intensity sustains persistence? Too little = technocratic capture. Too much = institutional collapse.

Research Agenda

Immediate Tasks (2–5 years)
  • → Empirical Validation: Replicate TARCH-X on traditional financial crises. Test belief-transfer in organizational capture.
  • → Formalization: Prove lumpability theorems. Derive stakes-weighted legitimacy from first principles.
  • → Policy Applications: Climate governance (COP as consent-holding failure), decolonization reparations, AI regulation.
  • → Cross-Disciplinary Training: Political theorists learn econometrics. Finance PhDs read Spivak and Fanon.
Long-Term Vision (10–20 years)
  • → Institutional Recognition: Adversarial Systems as named field with dedicated journals, cross-departmental programs.
  • → Paradigm Shift: Friction reframed as constitutive. Equilibrium models recognized as special cases.
  • → Testable Cosmology (Moonshot): Hubble tension directional variance, dark energy evolution. If validated = paradigm shift. If falsified = precise boundary on unification scope.
  • → Infrastructure: Shared datasets, open-source ROM libraries, reproducibility standards, collaborative platforms.

Implications and Stakes

Why This Matters

1. EXISTENTIAL RISK

AI alignment is a consent-holding problem, not a technical one. As AI capability scales, stakes rise faster than voice broadens. Friction accumulates. The alignment community that ignores political economy will build technically aligned systems that precipitate legitimacy crises.

Climate negotiations fail for the same reason: developing nations bear consequences (high stakes) without proportional influence (low voice). Adversarial systems perspective: legitimacy erosion is quantifiable, predictable, and avoidable if friction is managed structurally.

2. EPISTEMIC JUSTICE

Postcolonial critique identifies power asymmetries but lacks formalism. ROM provides it. Belief-transfer mechanism explains why decolonization is traumatic for colonizers—they genuinely perceive consent as owned after sustained delegation.

Formal models without normative grounding are technocratic violence. Adversarial systems demands both: equations and ethics.

3. INSTITUTIONAL RESILIENCE

Every major institution exhibits legitimacy erosion. The standard response: "more transparency," "stakeholder engagement." These fail because they're band-aids on structural friction.

Adversarial systems diagnosis: Competence-consent decoupling is inevitable under delegation. You need stakes-weighted voice mechanisms or institutional redesign.

The Wager

This research program makes a falsifiable empirical claim: ROM friction decomposition predicts observable outcomes across domains.

If TARCH-X replicates, if stakes-weighted legitimacy forecasts institutional crises, if trauma interventions grounded in adversarial training theory outperform affect-regulation protocols, if Hubble tension shows directional variance—then adversarial systems is vindicated as unified field.

The disciplines don't map to reality. Reality is adversarial all the way down.
