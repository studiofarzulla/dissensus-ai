01 · Ways in

What we’re looking for

We seek collaborators interested in rigorously testing the Axiom of Consent framework and the research around it. The objective is not consensus but productive dissensus — identifying failure modes and refining the formalisations. Academics, independent researchers, and the determinedly cross-disciplinary are all welcome: affiliation is optional, rigour is not.

Areas Consent mechanics·Machine morality·Economic pharmakon·Crypto microstructure·Political economy·ROM framework
I
Co-authorship
Joint papers on consent mechanics, machine morality, or economic microstructure. Empirical or theoretical.
II
Critical review
Formal critiques of our frameworks. We publish strong counterarguments with full attribution.
III
Empirical testing
Apply friction decomposition to new domains. Market data, political systems, agent simulations.
IV
Institutional partnership
University affiliations, research grants, doctoral supervision. Scaling beyond independent work.
02 · The distinction

Three doors, deliberately separate

Collaboration is the research door. If you want to fund the open programme, that is Partners; if you want to hire the methods for a commercial problem, that is Services. Keeping the three apart is how the lab stays independent.

Collaborate · this page
Research partnership: co-author, critique, test. Ideas move; money does not.
Partners · funding
You fund the open programme. Outputs stay public, citable, and everyone's.
Services · commercial
You bring a problem, we scope it, you own the deliverable. Invoices exist.
