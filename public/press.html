Boilerplate Text

Short Description (50 words)

Dissensus is a governance alignment research lab focused on adversarial systems—complex agent networks where equilibria are structurally impossible. We formalize friction dynamics across political economy, AI alignment, and financial microstructure, developing substrate-independent frameworks for measuring coordination overhead.

Long Description (150 words)

Dissensus is a governance alignment research lab investigating adversarial systems—any sufficiently complex dynamic network of agents where equilibria are impossible to reach due to formalized friction constraints. These systems function through friction reduction rather than friction elimination, making them structurally adversarial yet persistently functional.

Founded in 2026, the lab develops formal frameworks including the Axiom of Consent (a pre-game-theoretic framework for delegation analysis) and the Replicator-Optimization Mechanism (computational unity across biological and abstract substrates). Research spans consent mechanics, machine morality, economic microstructure, and political legitimacy quantification. All outputs are published open-access under CC-BY 4.0 licensing.
