Active research programme: ASCRI

The core agenda

Adversarial Systems & Complexity Research Initiative

Dissensus is built around a single ongoing programme. ASCRI investigates sufficiently complex dynamic networks of agents where equilibria cannot be reached under formalised friction constraints. Such systems function only through the continual reduction of friction: they are adversarial against themselves, yet persist through dissensus. Every paper in the archive probes one face of this programme, whether consent mechanics, market microstructure, machine morality, or the process-philosophical foundations underneath.

02 · The problem

Before strategy, there is delegation

Game theory presupposes participation. It does not account for how agents enter strategic interaction, why payoff structures take the forms they do, or what happens when participation is refused. It does not interrogate who defines the rules, whose interests they serve, or whether the game itself is legitimate.

We study the layer underneath. Before strategy, there is delegation. Before delegation, there is consent. Before consent, there is stake, someone's optimisation being transferred to another's control.

Friction decomposition
Friction = f(alignment, stake, entropy)

Alignment: convergence or divergence of optimisation targets between principal and agent.

Stake: the magnitude of optimisation being delegated (what is at risk).

Entropy: efficiency loss in the optimisation transfer (information degradation).

We formalise this friction. Not to eliminate it (friction is the cost of existence in an adversarial environment) but to measure it, and to make visible the invisible taxes that coordination imposes.
