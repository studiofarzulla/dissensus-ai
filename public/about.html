About

A small lab with a long thesis.

Dissensus is an independent UK research lab investigating adversarial systems — where competing interests generate structural conflict — through computational, empirical, and theoretical work. Friction dynamics is the through-line.

01 · Mission

Why the lab exists

Every system that delegates — markets, institutions, model deployments — pays a coordination cost. We think that cost has structure: it rises with what is at stake and with how badly preferences are misread, and it falls with how well interests align. Making that structure formal, measurable, and testable across domains is the entire research programme.

F = σ (1 + ε) 1 + α
The thesis in one line. Everything else in the archive is a domain-specific test of it.
The programme

ASCRI — Adversarial Systems & Complexity Research Initiative

The research is organised under ASCRI, hosted at systems.ac: one programme, many fronts. A paper on cryptocurrency volatility and a paper on machine ethics look unrelated until you notice they are both probing how systems behave when their parts want different things.

03 · Domains

Four fronts, one thesis

Each domain is a different place to catch the same phenomenon in the wild. Publications in all four are listed under Research.

Market microstructure, volatility asymmetry, systemic risk, event-study methodology.
02 · Political economy
Legitimacy quantification, institutional friction, governance and consent mechanics.

Multi-agent coordination, machine ethics, training dynamics as development.

The friction formalism itself — theorem-proved in Lean 4, tested in simulation.
